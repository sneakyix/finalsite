Article Summary

Google CEO Larry Page is selling Google Glass to anyone who can think of a unique use for the glasses and who can also afford the $1500 cost.

It is unsure of what role marketers will play on Google Glass. Google mentioned that they do not intend on displaying digital ads on Google Glass. Also it was mentioned at a conference that Google held that Twitter, Facebook, Tumblr, Evernote, CNN and Elle are building apps. Google's long term plans for Glass and it's advertising strategy is unclear.

Google has said that all glassware (software) on the device will be free. Google Glass initially has no locking system, meaning if the device was stolen everything on the device could still be accessed by anyone with no problem. So a technologist at Isobar developed an app called Bullet Proof that locks Google Glass as soon as the glasses are taken off.

So far there is 10 000 pairs of Google Glasses out, and are owned by as Google calls them "Explorers" who own the Glasses but are basically just testing them out. As of now, Google does not have an official release date for Google Glass. Although they have not announced a release date, this hasn't stopped people from dreaming about the great marketing abilities Glass will have. For example travel and hospitality could benefit because Glass could easily direct users to landmarks and other tourist attractions.
